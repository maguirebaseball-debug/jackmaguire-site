The NYC pizza conversation defaults to about ten names. Di Fara. Lucali. Scarr's. L'Industrie. Every few months a new spot blows up on TikTok and the line forms around the corner. By the time it hits your feed, it's gone from discovery to scene in about six weeks.

I wanted a different list. Places that are genuinely good by the numbers, not by the algorithm.

So I wrote scripts to query the Google Places API across 100+ NYC neighborhoods, filtering for pizza shops with 4.7 stars or higher and between 30 and 200 total reviews. The lower bound filters out places with too few ratings to trust. The upper bound is the rough cut-off for "hasn't blown up yet." The search ran neighborhood-by-neighborhood across all five boroughs: Astoria to Tottenville, Inwood to Rockaway Park, College Point to City Island.

I have to be upfront about something: I haven't eaten at most of these. I've spent years recommending restaurants to friends, with a strong track record, and I trust my read of the data. But these are research findings, not tasting notes. I'm publishing this because the list is useful, and because the spots that are currently under 200 reviews won't stay there.

---

## Why the Review Count Filter Matters

Under 30 reviews in New York City is noise. A few friends of the owner, one great week, a random influx from a local Facebook group. Not enough signal.

Over 200 reviews in New York City usually means someone wrote it up. Eater has found it. A food creator has posted it. The line starts forming. This is not a judgment, it's just how the math works: a place doing consistent, high-quality work in a city of 8 million people does not stay at 200 reviews forever.

The 30 to 200 range is the sweet spot. Enough reviews to establish a real pattern, not enough to have attracted the crowds that make discovery less fun. This list reflects late May 2026. Some of these will cross the threshold before you read this.

## Manhattan

**[Fermento Pizza NYC](https://www.fermentonyc.com/)** — 226 Varick St, West Village. 5.0 stars, 96 reviews.

A naturally fermented sourdough slice shop in the West Village, which means the crust actually has flavor before toppings. The style is Neapolitan-influenced, topped with things like burrata and artisanal meats. Perfect 5.0 with nearly 100 reviews is rare; this is the most statistically compelling entry on the Manhattan list.

**[Briciola Pizza Bar](https://www.briciolapizzabarnewyork.com/)** — 14 Bedford St, West Village. 4.9 stars, 117 reviews.

Brick oven pizza two blocks from Fermento, also in the West Village. The neighborhood has two serious contenders now and most people only know about one of them.

**[L'industrie Pizzeria, Little Italy](https://www.lindustriebk.com/)** — 197 Grand St. 4.9 stars, 163 reviews.

Worth flagging: L'industrie's original Williamsburg location is famous and packed. This third location, which opened in March 2026 right next to Ferrara Bakery, is not yet crowded. Same quality, dramatically different wait times. This is the rare case where name recognition works in your favor, because people assume it's already been discovered and skip it. It hasn't. Go now.

## Brooklyn

**[STUPIDROMANS](https://www.stupidromans.pizza/)** — 1063 Bedford Ave, Bed-Stuy. 4.9 stars, 114 reviews.

The name is doing a lot of work here. Old-school Brooklyn pizza ethos, owner grew up on the borough's slice shops. Margherita and Sicilian are the standbys. The branding is deliberately anti-aesthetic in a way that suggests they're confident in the product.

**[Third Time's the Charm](https://www.charmbarnyc.com/)** — 275 Van Brunt St, Red Hook. 5.0 stars, 69 reviews.

Sourdough pizza, cocktails, Red Hook. A lawyer opened this place, which either means nothing or explains the confidence. The Infatuation reviewed it; still under 70 reviews as of this writing, which means Red Hook's relative inaccessibility is doing the work of keeping it off the radar. Worth the trip.

**[Ace's Pizza Bushwick](https://www.acespizzaspot.com/)** — 423 Troutman St, Bushwick. 4.9 stars, 47 reviews.

Detroit-style square pies with crispy edges, which is still an underrepresented style in New York relative to how good it is. 47 reviews means this is early. Sunday through Thursday 12 to 11, Friday and Saturday until midnight.

**[888 Pizza](https://888pizzany.com/)** — 888 Wyckoff Ave, Bushwick/Ridgewood border. 5.0 stars, 32 reviews.

A perfect 5.0 with only 32 reviews is the highest-risk, highest-reward entry on this list. The review count is at the floor of what I'd consider statistically meaningful, but a 5.0 with no negative reviews at all in New York City is hard to fake. This one is worth the small gamble.

**[San Antonio's Wood Fired Pizza](https://sawfp.com/)** — 132 Eagle St, Greenpoint. 4.9 stars, 50 reviews.

Wood-fired Neapolitan. The catch: this is a Friday-only pop-up, 4 to 9 PM. The scarcity is probably part of why the ratings are so clean. Plan accordingly.

**[Rome to Brooklyn](https://www.rometobrooklynpizza.com/)** — 587 Manhattan Ave, Greenpoint. 4.9 stars, 169 reviews.

Pizza and panzerotti (deep-fried calzones) from chef Aurel Xhepexhiu, who opened the original Williamsburg location in 2020. The Greenpoint location is newer, less trafficked, and does grandma-style pies alongside the Roman-style rounds. Panzerotti are worth ordering once just to know what they are.

**[Impasto](https://www.impastopizzanyc.com/)** — 200 Garfield Pl, Park Slope. 4.9 stars, 104 reviews.

Roman-style square slices and 18-inch New York-style pies, on the corner of Garfield and 7th Avenue. Park Slope has no shortage of food options, which is probably why this one gets less attention than it deserves.

**[THE SWISS SLICE GT](https://maps.app.goo.gl/kJLkVxzk8Q9YW5Hm8)** — 2703 Church Ave, Flatbush. 4.9 stars, 67 reviews.

The all-caps name and the Church Avenue address put this firmly outside the standard food-media coverage zone, which is probably why it has 67 reviews. Flatbush pizza spots don't get written up. This one probably should be.

**[Amuni](https://amunibrooklyn.com/)** — 7217 3rd Ave, Bay Ridge. 4.9 stars, 146 reviews.

Sicilian-style in Bay Ridge, which is where you go when you want Italian-American food cooked for a neighborhood rather than for a write-up. Amuni has outdoor dining and a full Italian menu alongside the pizza.

## Queens

**[Freddy's Pizza](https://www.freddyspizzany.com/)** — 25-27 Broadway, Astoria. 4.9 stars, 83 reviews.

The original Freddy's opened in Whitestone in 1961. This Astoria location is the second, and newer, which means it gets the legacy quality without the legacy crowds. Classic New York slices, some funkier options (white truffle, Sicilian spicy vodka pepperoni), open every day 11 AM to 11 PM.

**[Donabella Restaurant Pizzeria](https://www.collegepointpizzarestaurant.com/)** — 18-12 College Point Blvd, College Point. 4.9 stars, 161 reviews.

College Point is one of those Queens neighborhoods that food coverage just doesn't reach. Donabella does brick oven pizza alongside pasta and seafood, opens at 10 AM daily, and has 161 reviews with no signs of being discovered by anyone outside the immediate area.

**[Seany Pizza](https://seanypizza.com/)** — 97-01 Shore Front Pkwy, Rockaway Park. 4.9 stars, 58 reviews.

Pizza on the boardwalk. The Beach 97th Street concession, open 7 days a week noon to 8 PM. The location is the story here. You're not going to Rockaway Park on a Wednesday for pizza, but if you're making the trip to the beach anyway, this is the answer to what you're eating.

## The Bronx

**[City Island Pizza Company](https://www.cityislandpizza.com/)** — 273 City Island Ave, City Island. 4.9 stars, 83 reviews.

City Island is a nautical enclave in the northeastern Bronx that most New Yorkers have never visited. This place leans into it: natural wines, Italian-American classics, a mid-century nautical atmosphere. Open Wednesday through Friday evenings, weekends from 3 PM. Going here is a commitment, which is exactly why it has 83 reviews and not 800.

---

## Staten Island

**[La Vera Pizzeria](https://laverapizzasi.com/)** — 2071 Clove Rd, Grasmere. 4.9 stars, 194 reviews.

Staten Island pizza is a legitimate regional tradition that New York food media mostly ignores because Staten Island is inconvenient to get to. La Vera is at 194 reviews, which means it's right at the edge of this list's criteria. I'm including it because it's the best-rated pizzeria in a borough that takes the food seriously and doesn't get enough credit for it.

## A Note on Timing

This list is a snapshot of late May 2026. Review counts move. The spots at the lower end of the range (888 Pizza at 32, Ace's at 47) have more runway. The ones approaching 200 may already have lines by the time you read this.

If you go somewhere from this list and it's turned into a scene, that's not my fault. That's what happens when a place is actually good.
