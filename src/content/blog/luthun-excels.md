I sought an exceptional dining experience and discovered Luthun, a restaurant in the neighborhood that had been frequently recommended on Reddit. Upon arrival, I was impressed by the clean, contemporary space with an open kitchen and attentive staff.

The sommelier engaged in thoughtful conversation about wine preferences. I progressed through several selections: an Argentine wine with high acidity and strong minerality for the opening course, a skin contact wine with light smokiness and depth for the second course, and a medium-bodied red with high acidity to accompany beef. A digestif concluded the wine pairing.

The lightly fried scallops stood out most: cooked precisely and rich in texture. The sauces were balanced and composed, supporting each dish without pulling focus. A frisée salad prepared with liquid nitrogen provided distinctive texture.

What made the meal was its coherence. The food, the wine, the pacing, and the room all felt guided by a steady point of view. Rather than leaving excited, I felt settled. The time had been well spent.

One of the best meals I have had in New York.
