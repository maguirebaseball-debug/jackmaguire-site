This is a research-verified list of events happening on Monday, May 25, 2026 outside Midtown Manhattan. Every entry has a start time, venue, and at minimum a neighborhood. Prices are listed where confirmed. Events are organized by type, then by borough, then by time of day.

A few notes on scope: the beach entries each count as distinct scenes with distinct community characters. The DanceAfrica entries span a full day across multiple formats. The parades are all neighborhood-organized, all free, all outside Midtown. The parties and restaurant events are independently operated, not corporate chains.

## Parades and Ceremonies

**Brooklyn Memorial Day Parade**
Bay Ridge, Brooklyn. Starts 11:00 AM at 78th Street and Third Avenue. Route proceeds south on Third Avenue to Marine Avenue, then north on Fourth Avenue to John Paul Jones Park for a memorial service: bagpipes, formal flag raising, wreath laying by veteran service organizations, and a 21-gun salute by the Veteran Corps of Artillery. Free. 159th annual.

**Green-Wood Cemetery Honor March**
Green-Wood Cemetery, Fifth Avenue entrance, Brooklyn. 11:00 AM. The march precedes the afternoon concert. Free.

**Green-Wood Cemetery Memorial Day Concert**
Green-Wood Cemetery, Brooklyn. 2:00 PM to 4:00 PM. The ISO Symphonic Band performs Sousa and Bernstein on the cemetery's rolling lawns, honoring composers and service members among the more than 570,000 interred. 26th annual. Free with suggested donation of $10 per person.

**Urban Park Rangers: Civil War History Walk**
Grand Army Plaza, Prospect Heights, Brooklyn. 11:00 AM to 12:30 PM. NYC Parks Urban Park Rangers lead a historical walk around Grand Army Plaza explaining the statuary, the origins of Memorial Day, and its connection to the Civil War. Free. Registration required at nycgovparks.org (opens May 18).

**Van Nest Memorial Day Parade**
Van Nest Avenue, Bronx. 12:00 PM to 1:00 PM. Organized by the Van Nest Neighborhood Alliance and the Bronx County American Legion. Route runs along Van Nest Avenue between White Plains Road and Victor Street, ending at Van Nest Memorial Plaza for a ceremony. Free.

**City Island Memorial Day Parade**
City Island Avenue, Bronx. 2:00 PM to 4:00 PM. Parade runs on City Island Avenue between Winter Street and Pilot Street. The entire island turns out. City Island is accessible by the BX29 bus from Pelham Bay Park station. Free.

**Rockaway Beach Memorial Day Parade**
Rockaway Beach Boulevard, Queens. 11:00 AM to 1:30 PM. Route runs between Beach 129 Street and Beach 130 Street. Free.

**Little Neck-Douglaston Memorial Day Parade**
Northern Boulevard, Queens. 2:00 PM to 4:30 PM. Route between Glenwood Street and 244th Street. Organizers describe it as the largest continuously-held Memorial Day parade in the nation. Free.

**Staten Island Memorial Day Parade**
Forest Avenue, Staten Island. Route runs from Hart Boulevard to Jewett Avenue. Free.

## Beaches Open for the Season

All eight NYC beaches open for swimming on Memorial Day weekend with lifeguards on duty daily from 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM through September 13. All are free. Each beach has a distinct community and cultural character.

**Coney Island Beach, Brooklyn**
The main Coney Island stretch: Russian and Ukrainian family setups, boardwalk vendors, the particular energy of a neighborhood that has been doing this for a hundred years. The boardwalk runs nearly three miles from Sea Gate to Brighton Beach. Lifeguards: 10 AM - 6 PM.

**Brighton Beach, Brooklyn**
Adjacent to Coney Island, almost entirely Russian and Ukrainian. Boardwalk vendors sell smoked fish, pickled vegetables, fruit, and baked goods in a format unlike any other NYC beach. The culture here is self-contained. Lifeguards: 10 AM - 6 PM.

**Manhattan Beach, Brooklyn**
Quieter, smaller, at the eastern tip of the Coney Island peninsula. Almost exclusively used by Sheepshead Bay, Gerritsen Beach, and Homecrest residents. Lifeguards: 10 AM - 6 PM.

**Rockaway Beach, Queens**
The broadest stretch of Atlantic-facing sand in the city. The A/C train and the S shuttle bring residents from across the boroughs. Local surfers hit the water at dawn before lifeguards post, then the beach fills with families. Boards are available for rent. Lifeguards: 10 AM - 6 PM.

**Jacob Riis Park, Queens**
National Park Service beach connected to the western end of Rockaway, with a 1932 Art Deco bathhouse designed by Robert Moses. More local than main Rockaway, no amusement strip, calmer. Parking fills by 9 AM; transit recommended. Lifeguards: 10 AM - 6 PM.

**Orchard Beach, Bronx**
The Bronx Riviera. 13 numbered sections, each with a different crowd. Sections 7-9 are loudest and most local, with salsa and merengue from speakers, vendors in the parking area, and a density that makes it unlike any other city beach. Lifeguards: 10 AM - 6 PM.

**Midland Beach / South Beach (Franklin D. Roosevelt Boardwalk), Staten Island**
Both open for the season. Staten Island beach culture is distinct from the other boroughs: lower density, higher local ownership, and a crowd that has been using these beaches for generations. Lifeguards: 10 AM - 6 PM.

## Governor's Island

**Governor's Island, Lower Manhattan**
Ferry departs from Pier 11 (Wall Street) and Pier 6 (Brooklyn Bridge Park). Operates 10 AM to 6 PM with ferries every 15 minutes during peak travel on the holiday. Car-free island 800 yards from Lower Manhattan: open lawns, hammock groves, art installations, food and drink vendors. The island is free to access; ferry tickets are separate. First full day of the summer season.

DanceAfrica is BAM's annual May festival celebrating African and African Diasporic culture through performance, community classes, and a free outdoor bazaar. This year's programming focuses on Uganda. All events are at or near BAM at 30 Lafayette Avenue, Fort Greene, Brooklyn.

- 11:00 AM: Inclusive interactive class led by Pat Hall. In-person $12; virtual $10.
- 12:00 PM: Immersive class in Ugandan rhythmic traditions and movement. $12.

**DanceAfrica Bazaar**
Streets around BAM, 30 Lafayette Ave, Fort Greene. 12:00 PM to 8:00 PM. More than 150 vendors offering African, Caribbean, and African American food, crafts, and fashion. Rain or shine. Free entry.

**DanceAfrica Performance: Uganda**
BAM Howard Gilman Opera House, 30 Lafayette Ave. 3:00 PM. Dance and music performance celebrating Ugandan culture. Tickets from $25 at the box office. Discounted tickets available through TDF for qualifying patrons.

## How to Use This List

The day runs roughly in three blocks. Morning is parades and beaches: all free, spread across every borough, most of them done by 2 PM. Afternoon is cultural events and the start of the party circuit: DanceAfrica runs from noon to 8 PM, Partake NYC BBQ runs 3 PM to 10 PM, and several of the restaurant specials have defined windows. Evening is live performance and nightlife, running from 5 PM through overnight.

The most concentrated zones are Fort Greene and DUMBO in Brooklyn (DanceAfrica bazaar plus Ippudo happy hour), Ridgewood on the Brooklyn-Queens border (Nowadays), and the LIC/Astoria section of Queens (Partake NYC, Code Astoria, Hush Lounge).

The parades are genuinely worth attending even if they are not the main reason you leave the house. The City Island parade in the Bronx and the Van Nest ceremony are both small, specific, and completely non-touristy. The Brooklyn parade in Bay Ridge is larger but still a neighborhood event.
