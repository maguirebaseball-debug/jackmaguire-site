# Edge Computing Sector Rankings & Analysis

## 1. Belden Inc. ($BDC)
- **Bottleneck**: Ruggedized industrial networking.
- **Thesis**: The data backbone for harsh environments. Belden owns the "hardened" switches and protocols (Hirschmann) that keep data flowing when standard IT hardware fails.
- **Moat**: Dominant position in industrial ethernet; deep legacy installed base.
- **Valuation**: P/E 17.5 (Value play in a growth sector).

## 2. Digi International ($DGII)
- **Bottleneck**: Mission-critical M2M gateways.
- **Thesis**: Providing the "unbreakable" link for remote assets. Their routers handle the high-vibration, high-temp environments of utility grids and oil fields.
- **Moat**: High switching costs; certified for critical infrastructure use.
- **Valuation**: PEG 0.97 (Growth at a reasonable price).

## 3. SiTime ($SITM)
- **Bottleneck**: MEMS-based timing (clocks).
- **Thesis**: Every edge device needs a heartbeat. SiTime's silicon timing is 100x more resistant to vibration and temp than quartz, making it the choice for 5G and industrial edge.
- **Moat**: 90% share of the MEMS timing market; 100+ patents.
- **Valuation**: Market Cap $18.8B (Pricey, but owns the chokepoint).

## 4. Ambarella ($AMBA)
- **Bottleneck**: Low-power AI vision inference.
- **Thesis**: The pure-play NPU for the edge. While NVIDIA dominates the cloud, Ambarella's CV-series chips provide the best performance-per-watt for autonomous vision.
- **Moat**: Proprietary CVflow® architecture optimized for computer vision.
- **Valuation**: P/E Negative (Pure growth/recovery play).

## 5. Applied Optoelectronics ($AAOI)
- **Bottleneck**: Edge optical transceivers.
- **Thesis**: High-speed fiber links for localized data centers. As AI moves to the edge, copper becomes a heat bottleneck; AAOI provides the optical solution.
- **Moat**: Specialized manufacturing for short-reach optical links.
- **Valuation**: Forward PEG 0.85 (Turnaround play).
